Hell’s Gate National Park

"Where Earth Whispers in Steam and Stone"

Located south of Lake Naivasha in Kenya, Hell’s Gate is one of the few national parks that allows walking, cycling, and rock climbing. The park’s rugged landscape, towering cliffs, gorges, and geothermal steams give visitors a rare peek into the heart of the Earth. It inspired the setting for Disney’s *The Lion King* — yes, Pride Rock has roots in Kenya!

Things to Do

  • Cycle through the park’s volcanic plains with zebras and giraffes by your side.
  • Hike the dramatic Ol Njorowa Gorge and discover natural hot springs.
  • Rock climb the iconic Fischer’s Tower — a true test of spirit and stamina.
  • Camp under starlit skies and listen to the whispers of geothermal steam vents.

Fun Facts

  • The park was named after the intense geothermal activity that causes steam to rise dramatically from the ground.
  • Hell’s Gate is a key source of inspiration for several film studios due to its Mars-like terrain.
  • The Olkaria Geothermal Station nearby provides a significant portion of Kenya’s electricity.
