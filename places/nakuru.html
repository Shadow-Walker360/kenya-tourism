Lake Nakuru National Park

The Pink Heart of Kenya

Welcome to Lake Nakuru

Nestled in the heart of Kenya’s Great Rift Valley, Lake Nakuru is a breathtaking alkaline lake famous for its endless flocks of pink flamingos and diverse wildlife. This UNESCO World Heritage site offers more than just beauty — it’s a sanctuary of hope, rebirth, and unspoiled wonder.

The park, though small, is teeming with life. From graceful rhinos to lounging lions, to the ever-curious baboons, Lake Nakuru is the perfect mix of calm and wild — a place where the skies shimmer pink at sunrise, and the water mirrors the soul of Africa.

Things to Do at Lake Nakuru

  • Bird Watching: Witness millions of flamingos paint the lake pink — one of nature’s grandest spectacles.
  • Game Drive: Spot rhinos, lions, giraffes, and the elusive leopard along scenic tracks.
  • Viewpoints: Visit Baboon Cliff and Lion Hill for panoramic views of the lake and surrounding escarpments.
  • Photography: Capture dawn’s golden light over the lake — it’s Instagram gold, no filter needed.
  • Picnics: Enjoy a peaceful picnic surrounded by pelicans, zebras, and gentle breezes.

Fun Facts About Lake Nakuru

  • 🦩 Over 1 million flamingos have been recorded at once — it looks like the water’s wearing a pink coat!
  • 🌋 Lake Nakuru was formed by ancient volcanic activity in the Rift Valley millions of years ago.
  • 🦏 It’s one of Kenya’s top rhino sanctuaries, with both black and white rhinos thriving here.
  • 💧 The lake’s high alkalinity supports microscopic algae — flamingos’ favorite snack.
  • 🎥 Lake Nakuru has featured in several wildlife documentaries, including BBC’s “Africa” series.
  • 🌅 At sunrise, the water literally glows — locals call it *“The Lake That Wears the Sun.”*

Hidden Gems Around Nakuru

💎 *Menengai Crater* — one of the world’s largest volcanic calderas, offering epic hikes and views. 💎 *Hyrax Hill Museum* — ancient archaeological sites revealing prehistoric human life. 💎 *Kariandusi Caves* — home to some of the earliest stone tools ever found in Africa.
