Mount Longonot

"Step into the Crater of Adventure"

Mount Longonot, a dormant volcano in the Great Rift Valley, towers over the Kenyan plains with its dramatic caldera and sweeping landscapes. Hikers and nature lovers come here to challenge themselves while being rewarded with one of the most stunning views in the country.

Activities You Can Do

  • Hiking the Summit: A challenging 6-8 km loop trail around the caldera with jaw-dropping views.
  • Photography: Capture panoramic Rift Valley shots, sunrise over the crater, and unique flora and fauna.
  • Bird Watching: Spot flamingos, ostriches, and eagles that soar over the plains below.
  • Nature Walks: Explore the lower slopes with guide-led tours highlighting volcanic soil and endemic plants.
  • Camping: Spend a night under the stars near the base — wake up to misty crater views.
  • Wildlife Viewing: Zebras, gazelles, and other wildlife roam freely on the plains around the volcano.
  • Trail Running: For adrenaline junkies, the crater rim offers the perfect challenge.
  • Educational Tours: Learn about the volcanic history of Kenya and geological formations of the Rift Valley.

Fun Facts

  • Mount Longonot last erupted in the 1860s, leaving behind a massive crater 8 km in circumference.
  • The name “Longonot” comes from the Maasai word “Oloonong’ot,” meaning “mountains of many spurs.”
  • The crater is about 3,200 meters above sea level — that’s high enough for some serious cardio!
  • Some of the rarest plants in Kenya grow only on Mount Longonot’s slopes.
  • The mountain offers one of the best Rift Valley sunrise views — often described as “otherworldly” by hikers.

Story of Adventure

Imagine waking up at dawn, the mist curling around the caldera, and the first rays of the sun hitting the volcanic rocks. You lace up your boots, start the trail, and with every step, the world below expands — acacia trees, lakes, and wildlife stretch endlessly. By the time you reach the rim, your lungs burn, your legs ache, but your heart bursts with awe. This is more than a hike — it’s a journey into Kenya’s wild heartbeat.

Many travelers leave Longonot transformed — carrying memories of the sky mirrored in volcanic craters and the sense that they’ve truly touched the soul of Kenya.
