Maasai Mara National Reserve

"The Theatre of the Wild"

The Maasai Mara — Kenya’s crown jewel — is where Africa beats in full rhythm. Rolling savannas, endless herds of wildebeests, and lions that rule the golden plains make it one of the most iconic wildlife destinations on Earth. The reserve is named after the Maasai people and the Mara River that cuts through its heart.

Things to Do

  • Witness the Great Migration — one of the “Seven New Wonders of the World.”
  • Take a sunrise hot air balloon safari over the sweeping plains.
  • Visit a traditional Maasai village to experience authentic culture and song.
  • Spot the “Big Five” — lions, elephants, buffalo, leopards, and rhinos — in one thrilling game drive.

Fun Facts

  • Over 1.5 million wildebeests cross from Tanzania’s Serengeti to the Mara every year in search of greener pastures.
  • The reserve spans approximately 1,510 square kilometers and connects seamlessly to the Serengeti ecosystem.
  • The Maasai people’s bright red shukas are said to symbolize courage and ward off lions.
